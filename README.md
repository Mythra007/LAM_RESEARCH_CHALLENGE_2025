
The aim of this project is to design, fabricate, and demonstrate arena with gates, LED display and;
1.	SARM (Single Arm Robot on Omni-Wheeled Platform) — remotely controlled via Bluetooth to detect, pick, and remove obstacles from the ALFR path.
2.	ALFR (Advanced Line Follower Robot) — autonomously navigates the arena, interacts with gates, triggers the peristaltic pump, LAM LED system, and completes the sequence with a final weight measurement.
The objective is to perform the entire workflow within 10 minutes, while demonstrating robotics integration, embedded systems, sensing, fluid control, and mechanical design.

Serial Bluetooth communication was used for ESP32 and phone communication. Open source libraries were used for Arduino NANO codes to use IR and Ultrasonic sensors to control ALFR; and to manipulated servo’s using Arduino. MIT app maker was used to make app for android to control SARM. 

For the Grand Finale,  developed a heat exchanger system maintaining a 20°C temperature difference.
